# ORACIA Safety Policy

ORACIA is a symbolic entertainment and self-reflection product. It must not be presented or operated as medical, psychological, legal, financial, relationship, career, or emergency-support advice.

## 1. User agency

All product copy, reports, emails, and support responses should preserve user agency. Avoid deterministic language such as:

- you must
- your destiny is
- this proves
- you are doomed
- this person is meant for you
- quit your job / leave your partner / make this investment

Preferred framing:

- consider journaling about
- one possible reflection prompt is
- this may help you notice
- you remain the decision-maker

## 2. Visible disclaimers

The following locations must include clear disclaimers:

- Home page before checkout
- Checkout consent checkboxes
- Success page
- Cancellation page
- Report body
- PDF footer
- Report delivery email
- Terms page
- Help page

## 3. Crisis and distress handling

If a user expresses immediate safety risk, self-harm intent, harm-to-others intent, severe distress, or crisis dependency, do not argue, interpret their report, upsell, or continue symbolic analysis. Direct them to local emergency services, a crisis hotline, a trusted person, or a qualified professional.

## 4. Purchase friction

The checkout flow should include:

- 18+ affirmation
- acknowledgement that ORACIA is not advice
- crisis-keyword block for form fields
- a rolling purchase cap per email
- clear refund path

## 5. Refund posture

Refund requests involving distress, regret, fear, dependence, compulsion, or misunderstanding should be handled promptly and without debate. Losing one order is preferable to increasing user distress.

## 6. Data deletion

Where technically and legally possible, support should honor customer data deletion requests. Some payment records may need to be retained for accounting, dispute, fraud prevention, or legal compliance.

## 7. Support SLA

Distress-related support emails should receive a response within 48 hours. The response should be brief, non-interpretive, and direct the user to appropriate support resources.

## 8. Known limitation

No disclaimer or rate limit can fully prevent misuse. The system should reduce foreseeable risk through repeated framing, friction, crisis resources, refunds, and deletion paths.
